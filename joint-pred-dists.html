From Predictions to Decisions: The importance of Joint Predictive Distributions

Though accurate marginal predictions are sufficient for some applications, in this paper, we show that for a broad class of downstream tasks, accurate joint predictions are essential. In particular, we show that even in simple combinatorial and sequential decision problems, accurate marginal predictions are insufficient for effective decisions. Rather, accurate joint predictive distributions are required to achieve good performance. We also show that, in the multi-armed bandit, a classical sequential decision problem, accurate joint predictive distributions enable near-optimal performance. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2107.09224
